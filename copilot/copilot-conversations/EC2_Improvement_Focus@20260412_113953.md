**ai**: Economies of Scale refer to the cost advantages that enterprises obtain due to their scale of operation, with cost per unit of output generally decreasing with increasing size of output.

Essentially, as a company produces more of a good or service, the cost of producing each individual unit tends to go down. This can happen for several reasons, including:

-   **Specialization:** Larger firms can afford to specialize labor and equipment, leading to greater efficiency.
-   **Bulk Purchasing:** Buying raw materials or components in larger quantities often leads to lower prices per unit.
-   **Fixed Cost Spreading:** Fixed costs (like rent, machinery, or research and development) can be spread over a larger number of units, reducing the fixed cost per unit.
-   **Technological Advantages:** Larger firms might be able to invest in more efficient technologies or production methods that are not feasible for smaller operations.
