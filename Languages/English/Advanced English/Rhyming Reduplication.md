# hoity-toity 
behaving like you are better than everyone else

>[!EXAMPLE]
>I hate all this hoity-toity

# boogie-woogie
dance

>[!EXAMPLE]
>Let's go to a disco next weekend and boogie-woogie!

# fuddy-duddy
someone who is old-fashioned

>[!EXAMPLE]
>- Don't be such a fuddy-duddy, come out tonight!
>- His outfit was a bit fuddy-duddy

# silly billy
What we say to children who aren't behaving in a sensible way

>[!EXAMPLE]
> Oh, you are a silly billy, take that bucket off your head!

# hocus-pocus
silliness or magical activity, often used to trick someone. 

>[!EXAMPLE]
>Just be honest and stop all this hocus-pocus!

# lovey-dovey
A romantic adjective for people who show public displays of affection. (e.g. PDAs)

>[!EXAMPLE]
>Mum and dad are far too lovey-dovey! They're always holding hands!

# helter-skelter
- type of slide in the playground.
- an adjective to describe hurrying and confusion.

>[!EXAMPLE]
>We had a helter-skelter journey to the show!

# numby-pamby
an adjective meaning weak, feeble, and excessively emotional. (It's a bit cruel)

>[!EXAMPLE]
>What a namby-pamby idea!

# willy-nilly
an adverb to describe doing something in an irregular way.

>[!EXAMPLE]
>- The new agency just sends its proposals willy-nilly.
>- Don't just do it willy-nilly, think about it first.

# jeepers creepers
an exclamation, showing surprise or shock 

>[!EXAMPLE]
>jeepers creepers, knock on the door before you just come in next time!

# Rhyming Shmyming

Replace the first consonant with **shm**. This is an intensifier to make the concept more memorable.

## money shmoney
- money's not important
- money's no object

## homework shmomework
- There are better things to do

## bored shmored
- Confrontation
